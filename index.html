Excellence & Cohesion in EU innovation policy
June 2026 · Ralf

The EU's proposed budget would steer research-and-innovation funding more by excellence and less by cohesion. Are the two really at odds? Using patent-level spillover value and returns-to-R&D, we compare the US, China and two EU groups — the below-average-income members (CFplus) and the rest — and find the lower-income members are not uniformly behind: in a distinct set of technologies their best innovations top the rest of the EU. Interactive technology explorer, a hyperlinked patent-holder word cloud, and an Espacenet-linked map throughout.

EMDERLT — the Emerging and Developing Economies Research Lab Tracker — an interactive world map
June 2026 · Ralf

1,468 verified R&D labs that the world's leading R&D-investing firms run in emerging and developing economies — including developing-country champions like Huawei, BYD, Alibaba, TCS, Embraer, Arçelik, Gree, Hikvision, SMIC and Sinopec. Two pipelines feed the map (toggle between them): firms from the EU R&D Scoreboard, and a new patent-holder pipeline that ranks the owners of developing-country (ex-China/India) innovation — surfacing domestic owners the scoreboard misses, such as Turkcell, Ford-Otosan, Petrobras/CENPES, CONICET, MIMOS and leading Brazilian, Mexican and South African universities. Hover any dot for what the lab does. The point: frontier corporate R&D reaches far beyond a few rich-world hubs.
